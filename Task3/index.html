Hospital 57357

What is Hospital 57357 ?

Hospital 57357, officially known as Childrens Cancer Hospital Egypt 57357 (CCHE 57357),
is one of the largest and most prominent pediatric cancer hospitals in the world.
Located in Cairo, Egypt, it was inaugurated in 2007 with the mission to provide free,
high-quality cancer treatment to children, regardless of their background or financial situation.
What makes 57357 unique is its complete reliance on donations from individuals, organizations, and institutions.
The hospital's number, "57357", comes from the bank account number used during its initial fundraising campaign,
which quickly became a symbol of hope and generosity across Egypt and the Arab world.
The hospital is equipped with state-of-the-art technology and follows the latest international medical standards.
In addition to treatment, it focuses heavily on scientific research, education, and the continuous training of
healthcare professionals, aiming to improve survival rates and overall care quality for pediatric cancer patients.
Hospital 57357 stands as a beacon of hope and a remarkable example of what can be achieved through community support,
innovation, and a commitment to saving children's lives.

Childhood Cancer

Childhood cancer is a serious health issue that affects thousands of children around the world every year.
Unlike adult cancers, childhood cancers are often not linked to lifestyle or environmental factors.
Instead, they typically develop due to changes in the child's genes or cells as they grow.
The most common types of childhood cancer include leukemia (cancer of the blood), brain tumors, and lymphomas.
These diseases can grow quickly, but many of them are highly treatable if diagnosed early and managed with the right medical care.
Treatment for childhood cancer often involves a combination of chemotherapy, radiation therapy, and sometimes surgery.
In many cases, children require long hospital stays and ongoing follow-up care.
While treatment can be challenging, advances in medicine have significantly improved survival rates.
Supporting children with cancer goes beyond medical treatment. Emotional, psychological,
and social support for both the children and their families is essential.
Hospitals like 57357 in Egypt are dedicated to providing this full circle of care for free,
giving hope to thousands of families.
Raising awareness, funding research, and ensuring access to high-quality care are key steps
toward beating childhood cancer and giving every child the chance to grow up healthy and strong.
